Kandy




Kandy,("Great City"), city in Sri Lanka's Central Highlands,
at an altitude of 1,640 feet (500 metres). It lies on the shore of
an artificial lake founded by the last Kandyan king, Sri Wickrama
Rajasinha,on the Mahaweli River (1807).
The most famous of its many Buddhist
temples is Dalada Maligava ("Temple of the Tooth"),
where since 1590 a sacred relic,
supposedly the Buddha's left upper canine,
has been preserved.



Ambalangoda



Ambalangoda is a sweaty, workaday town, entirely
overshadowed as a tourist destination by nearby Hikkaduwa.
The biggest reason to visit is to dig
under the Sri Lankan souvenir scene surface
and explore the enigmatic meanings behind the
ubiquitous 'devil' masks. Authentic devil dances,
which force out spirits causing illnesses,
often happen irregularly in villages
in the countryside.



Anuradhapura



Was the island's first capital founded in the
4th century BC. For 119 successive Singhalese Kings
it was the royal capital and lasted for around
1500 years. Several old buildings and landmarks still
exist in the hectares of this sacred property.
Anuradhapura is revered as one of this planet's
greatest monastic sites.' Anuradhapura's historic town
is a must stop.



Dambulla




The famous rock cave temple of Dambulla is an iconic
sight of Sri Lanka-you'll be familiar with its
beautiful Buddha-filled interior long before
you arrive in town. This remains an significant holy
place despite its somewhat commercial climate, and
should not be overlooked.



Sigiriya



The mysterious rocky outcrop of Sigiriya, rising
dramatically from the central plains, is perhaps
Sri Lanka 's single most spectacular sight.
Near-vertical walls rise to a flat-topped peak containing
the remains of an ancient civilization,
once believed to be the epicenter of Kassapa's short-lived
empire, and early in the morning there are stunning views
of mist-wrapped forests.



Knuckles Mountain Ranges



Knuckles range also known as Dumbara mountain range
is positioned off Kandy, in the northern
end of central Highlands of Sri Lanka.
To the south and east of the mountain range is
Mahaweli river basin at the same time as to the
west are Matale plains.



Arugam Bay



Arugam Bay is situated in the dry of Indian Ocean
in the Eastern Province. It had been a
famous tourist site in Sri Lanaka where most of the
tourists refer for water sports and sun bathing.
